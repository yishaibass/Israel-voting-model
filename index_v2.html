The matching challenge

Each ballot box needs a demographic profile. CBS publishes a file linking ballot boxes to statistical areas — but two complications arise:

Split boxes

When a polling station gets too many registered voters, the CEC splits it (box 3 becomes 3.1, 3.2, 3.3). CBS only records the original integer (3). Resolved by mapping decimal sub-boxes back to their integer parent.

SA boundary changes

CBS updated its statistical area map between 2011 and 2022. A conversion file bridges the two vintages. Where a 2011 SA split into multiple 2022 SAs, the ballot box is assigned to the largest 2022 fragment by population.

“Other” ballot boxes — excluded from model

Two categories of ballot boxes cannot be assigned demographics and are excluded from the regression entirely:

Envelope votes (absentee)

Soldiers at bases, hospitalised patients, travellers voting outside their registered station. No reliable demographic mix. Grew from 5.5% (K21) to 9.6% (K25) of valid votes.

Unmatched boxes (~84–103 per election)

Bedouin tribal areas, remote military camps, West Bank settlements — no CBS statistical area assignment. Small share (0.3–0.4% of valid votes). Coalition bibi share in these boxes is low (9–21%).
